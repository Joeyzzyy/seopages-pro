How to Write Alternative Page Copy That Converts

Copywriting frameworks, templates, and techniques to create alternative pages that turn comparison shoppers into customers.

TL;DR - Copywriting Rules

  • Hook with explicit comparison in your headline
  • Lead with fairness - acknowledge competitor strengths first
  • Use specific data - "47% faster" not just "faster"
  • Close with migration CTA to reduce switching friction

Writing alternative page copy requires a unique balance: you must acknowledge competitors fairly while positioning your product as the better choice. Done right, this honesty builds trust and drives conversions. Here's how to master alternative page copywriting.

According to Copyblogger's research on persuasive writing, comparison content that acknowledges alternatives builds 67% more trust than one-sided promotional copy.

The Alternative Page Copywriting Framework

1

Hook with the Comparison

Open with a headline that names both products. Example: "Notion vs Coda: Which Workspace Tool Fits Your Team?" Make the comparison explicit immediately.

2

Establish Credibility

Briefly mention your methodology: "We analyzed 50+ features, tested both tools for 30 days, and surveyed 200+ users." This builds trust for everything that follows.

3

Lead with Fairness

Acknowledge what the competitor does well before highlighting your advantages. "Competitor X excels at Y, but if you need Z, [Your Product] delivers."

4

Use Specific Data

Replace vague claims with specifics. Not "faster" but "47% faster load times". Not "cheaper" but "$29/mo vs $49/mo for equivalent features".

5

Close with Clear Action

End with a compelling CTA that references the comparison: "Ready to switch from [Competitor]? Start your free trial - we'll help you migrate."

Headline Formulas That Work

[Product A] vs [Product B]: Which [Category] Tool Wins in [Year]?

Example: "Slack vs Discord: Which Communication Tool Wins in 2026?"

[Number] Reasons to Choose [Your Product] Over [Competitor]

Example: "7 Reasons to Choose Notion Over Coda for Remote Teams"

Best [Competitor] Alternatives: [Your Product] + [Number] More

Example: "Best Mailchimp Alternatives: ConvertKit + 5 More Email Tools"

Key Copy Elements

Comparison Tables

Create clear, scannable tables with feature comparisons. Use checkmarks, X marks, and brief descriptions. Always be accurate.

User Testimonials

Include quotes from users who switched from the competitor. Real stories of migration success are highly persuasive.

Pricing Breakdowns

Show transparent pricing comparisons. Calculate total cost of ownership, including hidden fees the competitor might charge.

Migration Guides

Reduce friction by showing exactly how to switch. Step-by-step migration instructions address the "too much effort" objection.

Copywriting FAQ

How honest should I be about competitors?

Very honest. Modern buyers research extensively and will discover inaccuracies. Acknowledge competitor strengths, then explain why your solution is better for specific use cases. Honesty builds trust.

Should I mention competitor weaknesses?

Yes, but factually and professionally. Focus on limitations relevant to your target audience. Avoid personal attacks or subjective criticism. Let users verify claims themselves.

How do I handle pricing comparisons?

Be specific and fair. Compare equivalent tiers. If your pricing is higher, explain the value. If lower, avoid cheapening your brand - focus on value, not just cost savings.

What tone should I use?

Professional but confident. You're a trusted advisor helping users make the right choice - not a salesperson pushing your product. Educate first, sell second.
